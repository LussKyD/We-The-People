Article 1: Who's the Boss?

Simplified Summary: All sovereign power belongs to the people of Kenya. **You are the ultimate boss!** You can give this power to your leaders (like the President, MP, or MCA) to use for you, but they must always use it according to the Constitution.

Article 1. Sovereign power of the people.

  1. All sovereign power belongs to the people of Kenya and shall be exercised only in accordance with this Constitution.
  2. The people may exercise their sovereign power either directly or through their democratically elected representatives.
  3. Sovereign power under this Constitution is delegated to the following State organs, which shall perform their functions in accordance with this Constitution—
    1. Parliament and the legislative assemblies in the county governments;
    2. the national executive and the executive structures in the county governments; and
    3. the Judiciary and independent tribunals.

Chapter 2: Kenya's Identity (The Rules We Live By)

Simplified Summary: This chapter defines what makes Kenya *Kenya*. It establishes the **National Values** (like patriotism, human dignity, and rule of law) that **every single person and leader** in the country must follow. It also defines our official symbols like the flag, the National Anthem, and the Coat of Arms—these are things we all share!

Article 10. National values and principles of governance.

  1. The national values and principles of governance in this Constitution bind all State organs, State officers, public officers and all persons whenever any of them—
    1. applies or interprets this Constitution;
    2. enacts, applies or interprets any law; or
    3. makes or implements public policy decisions.
  2. The national values and principles of governance include—
    1. patriotism, national unity, sharing and devolution of power, the rule of law, democracy and participation of the people;
    2. human dignity, equity, social justice, inclusiveness, equality, human rights, non-discrimination and protection of the marginalised;
    3. good governance, integrity, transparency and accountability; and
    4. sustainable development.

Chapter 4: The Bill of Rights (Your Superpowers!)

Simplified Summary: This is your list of fundamental rights and freedoms that belong to **you** and cannot be taken away. Every person, from the President to a police officer, must respect these rights. It is the most important chapter for a young Kenyan to know.

Article 27: Equality and Freedom from Discrimination

This means you are equal to everyone else, and no one can treat you badly because of your background, religion, gender, or any other reason.

Article 33: Freedom of Expression

You have the right to say what you think and to share information. But remember: this right does not allow you to spread hate speech, propaganda for war, or incite violence.

Article 43: Economic and Social Rights

This guarantees that you have the right to essential services like **clean water**, **healthcare**, **housing**, and **education**. The government has a duty to work towards making these available to everyone.
