The Journey: Why the 2010 Constitution?

The 2010 Constitution is not just a document; it's a social contract born from a long struggle for justice, especially following years of **centralized presidential power** and the divisive **2007 post-election violence**. Kenyans demanded a system that would safeguard rights and ensure power was shared.

Key Contentious Issues in the 2010 Referendum:

  • Land Reform: Opposition came from large landowners who feared the reforms would challenge their holdings and address historical injustices.
  • Abortion Clause: Highly debated by religious groups who opposed the clause permitting abortion in cases of 'maternal health'.
  • Kadhi Courts: The inclusion of these courts (which settle Muslim civil matters) was controversial, raising questions about the secular nature of the state.

Despite these debates, the Constitution passed with **67% approval** and was promulgated on **27 August 2010** .

Preamble and Scope

The preamble sets the tone by emphasizing national unity, sovereignty, respect for the rule of law, human dignity, equality, social justice, and the aspirations of all Kenyans for a better future.

Bill of Rights (Chapter Four)

A cornerstone of the constitution, it guarantees civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights to all citizens. It includes rights to **equality, freedom of expression, fair trial, education, healthcare, labor rights, environmental protection**, and specifically protects marginalized groups including women, children, and persons with disabilities.

Citizenship

Defines who is a Kenyan citizen and criteria for acquiring citizenship by birth, registration, or naturalization. This includes the provision for **dual citizenship**.

The Three Arms of Government

The Executive

The executive branch consists of the **President** (Head of State and Government) and the **Deputy President**. Their main role is to implement laws and run the national government. The President appoints Cabinet Secretaries who run ministries but are not members of Parliament.

The Legislature

A **bicameral Parliament** is introduced composed of the **National Assembly** (primary law-making) and the **Senate** (protecting county interests). The legislature makes laws, oversees the executive, and represents the people.

The Judiciary

Establishes an **independent judiciary** with a Supreme Court, High Court, and subordinate courts. It guarantees judicial independence, access to justice, and interprets the law to ensure constitutional compliance.

Devolution (Counties)

One of the constitution’s key features, it creates **47 counties** with elected governors and assemblies. Counties manage local affairs including health services, agriculture, and infrastructure, **bringing governance closer to the people** and distributing resources more equitably (See **Fourth Schedule**).

Land and Environment

Land is classified into **public, private, and community land** with clear regulations. The constitution guarantees environmental rights and outlines sustainable management of natural resources, aiming to resolve historical land disputes.

Leadership and Integrity

Sets ethical standards for public officers, detailing values like **honesty, transparency, and accountability**. It establishes bodies like the Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission to oversee leadership integrity.

Finance, Security, and Elections

Public Finance

Defines principles for managing public funds transparently and responsibly. It establishes independent bodies for auditing and oversight.

Elections and Political Parties

Regulates **free and fair elections**, the conduct of political parties, and voter rights. It outlines the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC)’s role.

Security

Details the roles of security agencies (KDF, Police), ensuring their accountability to the constitution and civilian authority.

Implementation and Amendment Procedures

Sets processes for implementing the constitution, resolving conflicts, and amending provisions through parliamentary or popular initiatives ensuring flexibility and adaptability over time. The **Schedules** contain the technical rules, lists (47 counties), and oaths needed for the constitution to operate.

This constitution is a **transformative framework** that emphasizes inclusivity, the rule of law, and human rights, shaping Kenya’s democracy and development for future generations.
